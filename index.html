ABOUT ME

I am a second-year graduate student in the Asian Languages & Literature Department. My research cuts across the fields of literature and history in early medieval China, with a focus on funerary texts and their socio-cultural contexts. My work investigated the rarely explored realm of dirge writing in early imperial China, inviting scholarly attention to genre questions in literary writings as well as the impact of the specific socio-cultural context on the author-text-audience dynamic. Driven by my interest in digital humanities, I am pursuing the Graduate Certificate in Textual and Digital Studies. For my Capstone, I am working on getting my project web-published. This project aims at digitizing the famous Han dynasty(206BCE-220 CE) stele inscription "Inscription for Lord Jing"景君碑, as well as working on translating and annotating the text to reach a broader audience. During my free time, I enjoy exploring all sorts of new technologies to further my interests in graphic design, video editing, and blogging.
